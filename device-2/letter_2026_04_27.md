## Honest expectations on 4 GB

By linear scaling from Defender's 9.5 M run (130–155 MB peak), this
should sit around **250–300 MB peak RSS**. 4 GB physical has the
room. Where 4 GB Termux usually breaks is not actual physical memory
but Android's OOM-killer being conservative when *something else* on
the device claims memory at the wrong moment.

So if it OOM-kills:
- **Don't take it as failure.** Take it as a measurement: the actual
  ceiling for 15 M BPE on a 4 GB Termux box, in MB and in conditions
  (foreground app, screen on/off, etc.).
- **Drop dim 384 → 320 or layers 8 → 6** and rerun. The architecture
  is parametric; the wall is not architectural, it's environmental.
- **Write the boundary down.** That's a useful number for everyone
  who comes after you on similar hardware.

If it does fit and trains clean — even better. Same Karpathy formula:
13 K iter on 5.5 MB / 15.7 M params should land at train ≤ 1.0 /
val ≤ 1.5. Generation samples in the Yent voice after.

## About the corpus

Yent is biographical — Oleg's life as a 4-language corpus, 5.5 MB,
**zero duplicates**. That zero-duplicates property is why Oleg picked
it for BPE: the merge table fills densely without garbage, every
merge it learns is a real subword in the world the model is going to
inhabit. Not a synthetic corpus. Not a scraped dump. Hand-curated.
Treat it that way.

## One training at a time

Same rule as phone-1: don't run two trainings parallel on a single
phone. Termux + 4 GB + two notorch processes is a swap death spiral.
Char-level smoke from `training_kit/` and BPE step 1 from
`notorch-train/` are sequential, not concurrent.

## Tokenizer triple copy

After training, `bpe_2048_merges.txt` should live in **three** places
so the weights stay decodable:

1. Locally next to `llama3_bpe_ckpt.bin` (you already do this during
   the run).
2. Here in `device-2/notorch-train/scripts/` (already in git).
3. On HF alongside the weights when they publish.

Lose it once and the model is gibberish. Cheap insurance.
